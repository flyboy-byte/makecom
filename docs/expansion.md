## Why this doc exists

This packet has never been shown to anyone outside this conversation. Five specific
people were identified as the first to see it. Sending a link to five people with
different stakes and different expertise, all at once, with the same message, wastes
the one advantage this project actually has: two of the five can give something no
amount of research replaces — a real operator's reaction. That's worth planning for
rather than defaulting to a group text.

This is also, plainly, [Phase 2](../FRAMEWORK.md) — the primary-research step every
other document has been waiting on. Treat responses as findings, not just reactions:
they belong back in [`feasibility.md`](./feasibility.md) or
[`entrepreneur-notes.md`](./entrepreneur-notes.md) once gathered, the same way the
external research passes were merged in.

## The five, and what each is actually worth

**1. The Make.com retainer friend.** Runs a comparable business right now. The single
highest-value conversation available — not because they'll be encouraging, but because
they can confirm or kill the packet's central finding in one sentence: whether the
mandatory-retainer default is really a liability, or whether it works fine in practice
and the research (competitor pricing pages, not lived experience) missed something.
**Ask:** does the retainer-optional framing in [`what-to-sell.md`](./what-to-sell.md)
match what you actually see? What do clients push back on that never shows up on a
pricing page?

**2. The AI-phone-number retainer friend.** Even more directly relevant than #1 — a
retainer AI service handling business phone lines is close to the missed-call SMS
workflow specifically, the one flagged in [`risks.md`](./risks.md) as sitting in
unsettled TCPA law. This person may already know, operationally, whether that legal risk
is real or overstated, and how a live retainer business actually prices and churns.
**Ask:** how do you price the retainer, what makes clients cancel, and have you run into
consent/compliance friction on outbound texts or calls?

**3. The coding friend who uses Claude.** Not a market-validation conversation — a
process one. They're the audience for [`method.md`](./method.md) and the
`idea-to-business` skill specifically: does the method hold up to someone who'd actually
use it, and is there anything in the build (the site, the research-handoff workflow)
worth them stealing for their own projects.

**4. The business-owner uncle.** General business judgment from someone with real P&L
experience, not this specific niche. Most useful for the question this packet has
struggled to ask itself plainly: does this describe a business, or a job — see
[`feasibility.md`](./feasibility.md)'s economics. An experienced owner will smell that
distinction fast.

**5. The best friend, low business background, likes money ideas.** The lowest-stakes and
most useful comprehension test: does the single-page site actually communicate to someone
with zero context, or does it read like it needs the five people above to parse it. If
it doesn't land with this person in under two minutes, the page has a problem regardless
of what the other four say.

## The plan (to work out later, not decided yet)

- **Order:** operators (1, 2) before the others — their answers might change what's even
  worth showing #4 and #5. Send to #3 anytime, since their read doesn't depend on the
  others'. Send to #5 last, once the page itself feels settled, since their reaction is
  a test of the page more than the idea.
- **Framing per person, not one link.** Especially #1 and #2 — ask them to poke holes,
  not to admire it. Sending a peer/adjacent-business-owner a cold link to something that
  looks like a pitch risks reading as competitive rather than as a request for their
  actual expertise.
- **Specific questions ready before sending**, not "thoughts?" — the questions above are
  a starting point, not the final ask.
- **Capture responses somewhere durable.** Once real conversations happen, they're
  exactly the evidence [`FRAMEWORK.md`](../FRAMEWORK.md) Phase 2 has been blocked on —
  merge findings into the docs the same way the external research passes were, including
  the outcome even if it's negative.

Nothing above has happened yet. This is the plan to execute when picked back up.
